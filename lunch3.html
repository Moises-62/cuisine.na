Beef Burrito

Overview

This recipe provides instructions for making a simple, satisfying burrito packed with seasoned ground beef, melted cheese, and tender rice, all wrapped snugly in a warm tortilla. It's a quick and customizable meal perfect for lunch or dinner.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • Ground Beef (500g / 1 lb)
  • Taco Seasoning (1 packet or 2 tbsp)
  • Water (1/2 cup)
  • Onion (1/2 medium, chopped)
  • Garlic (2 cloves, minced)
  • Shredded Cheese (1 cup)
  • Large Flour Tortillas (4-6 pieces)
  • Cooked Rice (1.5 cups)
  • Refried Beans (1 cup, optional)
  • Cooking Oil (1 tbsp)

Steps:

  1. Brown the Beef Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 3 minutes. Add the ground beef and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until fully browned. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Season the Meat Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Stir in the taco seasoning and water. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 5 to 7 minutes, or until the liquid has mostly evaporated and the meat is well-coated.
  3. Prepare the Tortillas Warm the tortillas individually in a dry pan, a microwave, or a steamer for about 15-20 seconds each. This makes them pliable and easier to roll without tearing.
  4. Assemble the Burrito Lay one warmed tortilla flat on a clean surface. In the center, layer the cooked rice first, then spread on refried beans (if using).
  5. Add the Filling Spoon a generous amount of the seasoned ground beef mixture over the rice and beans.
  6. Add Cheese and Toppings Sprinkle shredded cheese and any desired cold fillings (like salsa or sour cream) onto the meat.
  7. Fold the Burrito Fold the sides of the tortilla inward about an inch. Then, fold the bottom edge up over the filling, tucking it in tightly. Roll the burrito upward to completely seal the contents.
  8. Serve Immediately Serve the burritos warm, or grill them seam-side down in a dry skillet for 1-2 minutes to get a crispy exterior.
