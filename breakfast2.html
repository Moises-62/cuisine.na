Pancake

Overview

Pancakes are a beloved classic breakfast food, made from a batter of flour, eggs, milk, and a leavening agent (like baking powder). The batter is cooked on a hot griddle or frying pan until golden brown. They are typically served stacked with butter, syrup, or fruit.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • Flour (1 cup all-purpose)
  • Milk (1 cup)
  • Egg (1 large)
  • Baking Powder (2 teaspoons)
  • Sugar (2 tablespoons)
  • Salt (1/2 teaspoon)
  • Butter (2 tablespoons, melted, plus extra for the griddle)

Steps:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In a separate medium bowl, whisk the egg and milk until those wet ingredients are well combined.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and add the melted butter. Whisk until just combined; the batter should still be slightly lumpy—do not overmix it.
  4. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or large non-stick frying pan over medium heat.
  5. Scoop 1/4 cup of batter onto the hot griddle for each individual pancake.
  6. Cook the pancakes for 2 to 3 minutes, or until bubbles appear on the surface and the edges look dry, then flip them over.
  7. Cook for another 1 to 2 minutes until the second side is golden brown.
  8. Remove the finished pancakes from the griddle and serve them immediately with butter, maple syrup, or your favorite toppings.
