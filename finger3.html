Lumpiang Shanghai

Overview

Lumpia Shanghai consists of thin, delicate wrappers tightly rolled around a savory filling of seasoned ground pork, carrots, and onions. The rolls are deep-fried until perfectly golden and crunchy. The small size makes them an ideal appetizer or side dish, traditionally served with a sweet and sour sauce.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • Ground Pork (500g / 1 lb)
  • Carrot (1/2 cup, minced)
  • Onion (1/2 cup, minced)
  • Garlic (2 cloves, minced)
  • Green Onion (1/4 cup, chopped)
  • Egg (1 medium, beaten)
  • Soy Sauce (1 tbsp)
  • Sesame Oil (1 tsp)
  • Lumpia Wrappers (40-50 pieces)
  • Cooking Oil (for frying)
  • Salt and Black Pepper (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Filling In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, minced carrot, minced onion, minced garlic, chopped green onion, beaten egg, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and black pepper. Mix everything thoroughly using your hands or a spoon until the ingredients are evenly distributed.
  2. Prepare the Wrappers Separate the lumpia wrappers carefully. Lay one wrapper flat on a clean, dry surface, positioning it like a diamond (a point facing you).
  3. Roll the Lumpia Place about 1 to 1.5 tablespoons of the meat filling near the point closest to you. Shape the filling into a short, thin log.
  4. Seal the Wrapper Fold the bottom point up over the filling. Fold the left and right corners inward. Then, roll the wrapper tightly upward toward the far point. Use a dab of water to moisten the far corner and press it down to seal the roll completely.
  5. Fry the Lumpia Heat the cooking oil in a deep pot or skillet over medium heat until it reaches about 350°F (175°C). Fry the lumpia in batches for 5 to 7 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Flip them often to ensure even cooking.
  6. Drain and Serve Remove the fried lumpia with tongs or a slotted spoon and drain them on a wire rack or paper towels to remove excess oil. Serve the Lumpia Shanghai hot with sweet and sour sauce on the side.
