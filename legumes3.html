Falafel

Overview

Falafel are savory, crispy fritters made from soaked (never cooked) chickpeas, fresh herbs (parsley and cilantro), and spices. The mixture is shaped into small balls or patties and deep-fried until dark golden brown and crunchy. Falafel is commonly served tucked inside pita bread with fresh vegetables and tahini sauce.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • Dried Chickpeas (2 cups, soaked)
  • Onion (1 medium, chopped)
  • Garlic (4-6 cloves)
  • Fresh Parsley (1 cup, packed)
  • Fresh Cilantro (1/2 cup, packed)
  • Flour (2 tbsp)
  • Baking Powder (1 tsp)
  • Cumin Powder (1 tbsp)
  • Coriander Powder (1 tsp)
  • Salt and Black Pepper (to taste)
  • Cooking Oil (for deep frying)

Steps:

  1. Soak the Chickpeas Rinse the dried chickpeas and soak them in a large bowl of cold water overnight (at least 12 hours). They should double in size. Drain the soaked chickpeas thoroughly—do not cook them.
  2. Prepare the Mixture In a food processor, combine the drained chickpeas, roughly chopped onion, garlic cloves, parsley, and cilantro. Pulse the mixture until it is coarsely ground, resembling coarse sand or breadcrumbs. Do not over-process into a paste.
  3. Season and Rest Transfer the mixture to a bowl. Stir in the flour, baking powder, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and black pepper. Mix well. Cover the bowl and refrigerate the mixture for at least 30 minutes to help the balls hold their shape.
  4. Shape the Falafel Use a spoon, a small ice cream scoop, or a specialized falafel scoop to form the mixture into small, uniform balls or patties (about 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter).
  5. Heat the Oil Heat the cooking oil in a deep pot or skillet to 350°F (175°C). Use an oil thermometer to ensure the temperature is consistent.
  6. Fry the Falafel Carefully drop the falafel into the hot oil in batches, ensuring you do not overcrowd the pot. Fry for 3 to 5 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are deep golden brown and crispy on all sides.
  7. Drain and Serve Remove the falafel using a slotted spoon and drain them on a wire rack or paper towels to remove excess oil. Serve the falafel immediately with tahini sauce, hummus, and pita bread.
