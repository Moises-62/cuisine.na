Fried Chicken

Overview

Classic fried chicken features pieces of chicken that are marinated or soaked, then dredged in a seasoned flour mixture and deep-fried until the skin is shatteringly crisp and golden brown, while the meat remains juicy and tender. The key steps are the buttermilk soak for tenderness and double-dredging for a thick, crunchy crust.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • Chicken (1.5 kg, assorted pieces)
  • Buttermilk (2 cups)
  • All-Purpose Flour (2 cups)
  • Cornstarch (1/2 cup)
  • Salt (2 tbsp total)
  • Black Pepper (1 tbsp)
  • Paprika (1 tbsp)
  • Garlic Powder (1 tbsp)
  • Onion Powder (1 tsp)
  • Cayenne Pepper (1/2 tsp, optional)
  • Peanut Oil or Vegetable Shortening (for frying)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Brine In a large bowl, mix the buttermilk, 1 tablespoon of salt, and hot sauce (if using). Add the chicken pieces, ensuring they are fully submerged. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or up to 24 hours.
  2. Mix the Coating In a separate wide, shallow dish, whisk together the flour, cornstarch, 1 tablespoon of salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne (if using).
  3. Dredge the Chicken Remove the chicken pieces from the buttermilk (do not rinse). Shake off any excess liquid. Place the chicken piece into the seasoned flour mixture, coating thoroughly.
  4. Double-Dip for Crispness Dip the coated chicken piece back into the buttermilk briefly, then immediately dredge it back into the seasoned flour mixture for a second, final coat. Press the flour mixture onto the chicken firmly to create a thick crust.
  5. Heat the Oil In a deep pot or Dutch oven, heat the oil over medium-high heat until it reaches 325°F (165°C). Use a thermometer to monitor the temperature.
  6. Fry the Chicken Carefully place 3-4 chicken pieces into the hot oil, ensuring you do not overcrowd the pot. Fry the dark meat (thighs, legs) for about 12-14 minutes, and white meat (wings, breasts) for 6-8 minutes, flipping occasionally.
  7. Check Doneness and Drain The chicken is done when the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C). Remove the chicken and place it on a wire rack over paper towels to drain excess oil.
  8. Serve Serve the fried chicken hot and fresh for the best crispy texture.
