Hotsilog

Overview

Hotsilog is a popular and hearty Filipino breakfast meal. The "Silog" meals are defined by their combination of three components: Hotdog (usually sliced and pan-fried), Sinangag (garlic fried rice), and Itlog (a sunny-side up or over-easy fried egg). It is traditionally served with a side of vinegar-garlic dipping sauce.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • Cooked Rice (2 cups, day-old rice preferred)
  • Garlic (4-5 cloves, minced)
  • Filipino-style Hotdogs (4 pieces)
  • Eggs (4 large)
  • Cooking Oil (for frying)
  • Salt (to taste, for rice and eggs)
  • Vinegar (optional, for dipping sauce)
  • Tomato slices (optional garnish)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Hotdogs Slice the hotdogs diagonally or lengthwise and fry them in a lightly oiled pan until they are slightly curled and browned. Remove them from the pan and set aside.
  2. Cook the Eggs In the same pan, add a bit more oil and fry the eggs until the whites are set but the yolks are still runny (sunny-side up or over-easy). Season lightly with salt and pepper, then set aside.
  3. Prepare the Garlic Fried Rice (Sinangag) In the pan used for the hotdogs and eggs, add the minced garlic and sauté in oil over medium heat until fragrant and light brown, being careful not to burn it.
  4. Fry the Rice Add the cold, day-old cooked rice to the garlic oil. Break up the clumps with a spatula and fry for 3 to 5 minutes, mixing well to coat the rice evenly with the garlic and oil.
  5. Season the Rice Season the Sinangag with salt to taste and mix well.
  6. Assemble the Dish Place a serving of the Sinangag (garlic fried rice) on a plate. Arrange the fried hotdogs and the fried egg (itlog) beside the rice.
  7. Serve Serve immediately, optionally with tomato slices and a side of vinegar for dipping the hotdogs.
