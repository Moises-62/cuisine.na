Caesar Salad

Overview

The Caesar Salad is a simple yet elegant salad known for its distinctive dressing made from raw egg, anchovies, lemon juice, olive oil, and Parmesan cheese. When tossed with fresh romaine lettuce and homemade croutons, it creates a satisfying balance of textures and flavors.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • Romaine Lettuce (1 large head)
  • Bread (2 slices, for croutons)
  • Egg Yolk (1 large)
  • Garlic (2 cloves, minced)
  • Anchovy Fillets (2, mashed)
  • Dijon Mustard (1 tsp)
  • Lemon Juice (2 tbsp, fresh)
  • Olive Oil (1/2 cup)
  • Worcestershire Sauce (1/2 tsp)
  • Parmesan Cheese (1/2 cup total, grated and shaved)
  • Salt and Black Pepper (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Make the Croutons Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Toss the cubed bread with 2 tablespoons of olive oil, garlic powder, and salt. Spread on a baking sheet and bake for 8 to 12 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy. Set aside to cool.
  2. Prepare the Dressing Base In a medium bowl, whisk together the egg yolk, minced garlic, anchovy paste, Dijon mustard, and lemon juice until well combined.
  3. Emulsify the Dressing Slowly drizzle in the 1/2 cup of olive oil while whisking continuously and vigorously. This creates a thick emulsion. Stir in the Worcestershire sauce and the grated Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  4. Prepare the Lettuce Wash and thoroughly dry the romaine lettuce (this is crucial, as wet lettuce dilutes the dressing). Tear the leaves into bite-sized pieces.
  5. Toss the Salad In a large bowl, place the romaine lettuce and the cooled croutons.
  6. Dress the Salad Drizzle half of the prepared Caesar dressing over the lettuce and croutons. Toss gently to coat. Add more dressing as needed, being careful not to over-dress the salad.
  7. Garnish and Serve Transfer the salad to a serving bowl. Garnish generously with shaved Parmesan cheese and serve immediately.
