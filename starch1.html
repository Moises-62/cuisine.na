Potato salad

Overview

Classic Potato Salad features tender boiled potatoes mixed with hard-boiled eggs, crunchy celery, onion, and a rich, creamy dressing based on mayonnaise and mustard. The key is seasoning the potatoes while they are still warm to absorb maximum flavor.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • Potatoes (1 kg, Yukon Gold or Red)
  • Eggs (4 large, hard-boiled and chopped)
  • Celery (3 stalks, diced)
  • Red Onion (1/4 cup, chopped)
  • Mayonnaise (1 cup)
  • Yellow Mustard (2 tbsp)
  • Vinegar (1 tbsp)
  • Sweet Pickle Relish (2 tbsp, optional)
  • Fresh Dill or Parsley (2 tbsp, chopped)
  • Salt and Black Pepper (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Potatoes Wash and peel the potatoes (or leave the skins on for red potatoes). Cut them into roughly 1-inch cubes.
  2. Boil the Potatoes Place the cubed potatoes in a large pot and cover them with cold water. Add 1 tablespoon of salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 to 15 minutes, or until the potatoes are easily pierced with a fork but not falling apart.
  3. Drain and Season Drain the potatoes completely. Immediately transfer them to a large bowl and drizzle the vinegar over the hot potatoes. Toss gently. Season lightly with salt and pepper while they are still warm to allow the flavor to soak in. Let them cool completely.
  4. Mix the Dressing In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, yellow mustard, and sweet pickle relish (if using) until smooth.
  5. Assemble the Salad Once the potatoes are completely cool, add the chopped hard-boiled eggs, diced celery, and chopped red onion to the bowl.
  6. Combine and Chill Pour the dressing over the potato mixture. Add the chopped fresh dill or parsley. Gently fold everything together until all ingredients are evenly coated.
  7. Serve Garnish Transfer the potato salad to a serving dish. Cover and chill in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours (or preferably overnight) for the flavors to fully meld. Garnish with a sprinkle of paprika before serving.
