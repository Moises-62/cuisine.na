Pork Kare-Kare

Overview

Pork Kare-Kare is a hearty Filipino stew featuring tender cuts of pork simmered in a thick, golden sauce made primarily from ground peanuts or peanut butter and toasted rice. It is characterized by its signature savory, slightly sweet flavor. Crucially, it must be paired with Bagoong Alamang (shrimp paste) on the side, as the bagoong provides the necessary salty contrast to the rich sauce.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • Pork Hocks or Shoulder (1.5 kg)
  • Water (approx. 8-10 cups)
  • Annatto Seeds (2 tbsp, or 1 tsp powder)
  • Onion (1 large)
  • Garlic (6 cloves, minced)
  • Peanut Butter (1 cup, smooth)
  • Toasted Ground Rice (2 tbsp, optional thickener)
  • Fish Sauce (1 tbsp)
  • Eggplant (2 medium)
  • Bok Choy / Pechay (1 large bundle)
  • String Beans / Sitaw (1 cup)
  • Banana Blossoms / Puso ng Saging (1 cup)
  • Cooking Oil
  • Bagoong Alamang (Shrimp Paste, for serving)
  • Salt (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Pork In a large pot, cover the pork hocks or shoulder with water. Add the quartered onion and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat, cover, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the pork is very tender. Skim off any impurities that rise to the top.
  2. Prepare the Annatto Water Soak the annatto seeds in 1/2 cup of warm water for 10 minutes, then mash the seeds to release the color. Strain the liquid and discard the seeds.
  3. Sauté the Aromatics Heat oil in a large separate pan. Sauté the minced garlic until fragrant, then add 1 tablespoon of fish sauce.
  4. Build the Sauce Stir the peanut butter into the pan with the garlic. Gradually add the annatto liquid and about 2 cups of the strained pork broth, stirring constantly until the sauce is smooth and creamy.
  5. Thicken the Sauce If using, whisk in the toasted ground rice for added thickness and texture. Simmer the sauce for 5 minutes.
  6. Combine Pork and Sauce Transfer the tender pork pieces from the pot to the sauce pan. Bring the mixture to a slow simmer, allowing the pork to absorb the flavors for about 10 minutes.
  7. Add Vegetables Add the tougher vegetables first (string beans and banana blossoms). Cook for 3 minutes. Then add the eggplant and bok choy/pechay. Cook the vegetables for only 2-3 minutes, until they are bright and tender-crisp.
  8. Serve Season the stew lightly with salt if needed. Serve the Pork Kare-Kare hot in a large bowl, always with the Bagoong Alamang (shrimp paste) served separately on the side.
