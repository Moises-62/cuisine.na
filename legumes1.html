Baked beans

Overview

Baked Beans are a dish of navy beans (or other white beans) slow-cooked in a rich sauce that typically includes molasses, brown sugar, mustard, and often bacon or cured pork for deep, smoky flavor. The slow baking process allows the flavors to meld beautifully, resulting in a thick, sweet, and savory side dish.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • Dried Navy Beans (450g / 2 cups)
  • Bacon or Salt Pork (150g, diced)
  • Onion (1 medium, chopped)
  • Garlic (3 cloves, minced)
  • Ketchup (1/2 cup)
  • Molasses (1/4 cup, dark)
  • Brown Sugar (1/4 cup, packed)
  • Apple Cider Vinegar (2 tbsp)
  • Worcestershire Sauce (1 tbsp)
  • Dry Mustard Powder (1 tsp)
  • Salt and Black Pepper (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Beans Rinse the navy beans and soak them in a large bowl of cold water overnight, or use a quick soak method (boil for 2 minutes, then let stand for 1 hour). Drain and rinse the soaked beans.
  2. Pre-Cook the Beans Place the soaked beans in a pot and cover them with fresh water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer gently for 45 to 60 minutes, or until the beans are tender but not mushy. Drain the beans, reserving the cooking liquid.
  3. Prepare the Sauce Base Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). In an oven-safe pot or Dutch oven, cook the diced bacon or salt pork over medium heat until crispy. Remove the pork/bacon pieces and set aside, reserving the fat in the pot.
  4. Sauté the Aromatics Add the chopped onion to the rendered fat and sauté until softened (about 5 minutes). Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Mix the Sauce Stir in the ketchup, molasses, brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, and dry mustard powder. Season with salt and pepper. Bring the sauce mixture to a gentle simmer.
  6. Combine and Bake Stir the drained pre-cooked beans into the sauce. Add the reserved cooked bacon/pork pieces. Pour in enough reserved bean liquid (or water) to just cover the beans.
  7. Slow Bake Cover the pot with a lid and bake for 2 to 3 hours, stirring every hour and adding more liquid if the beans look too dry. The beans are done when the sauce is thick and the beans are very tender. Serve hot.
