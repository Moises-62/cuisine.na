Graham Cake

Overview

Mango Graham Cake is a simple yet decadent Filipino icebox cake. It consists of layers of crushed or whole graham crackers, a rich, creamy mixture of sweetened condensed milk and all-purpose cream, and slices of fresh, ripe mangoes. It requires no baking—just freezing or extended chilling to set.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • All-Purpose Cream (2 large packs, chilled)
  • Sweetened Condensed Milk (1 can, chilled)
  • Graham Crackers (1-2 packs, whole)
  • Fresh Ripe Mangoes (3-4 large, sliced)
  • Butter (2 tbsp, melted, optional)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Cream In a large bowl, combine the two packs of chilled all-purpose cream and the can of chilled sweetened condensed milk. Beat the mixture using a hand mixer or whisk until light, smooth, and slightly fluffy (about 3-5 minutes).
  2. Prepare the Pan In an 8x8 inch square dish or loaf pan, lay a single layer of whole graham crackers to cover the bottom entirely. (Optional: Mix crushed crackers with melted butter to form a firm crust on the bottom layer.)
  3. First Cream Layer Spread one-third of the cream mixture evenly over the graham cracker base.
  4. First Fruit Layer Arrange half of the sliced mangoes neatly over the cream layer.
  5. Second Graham Layer Place another single layer of graham crackers over the mangoes.
  6. Repeat the Layers Spread the second one-third of the cream mixture over the crackers, then arrange the remaining mango slices on top.
  7. Final Layer Cover the mangoes with the remaining cream mixture. For the final topping, you can either crush the remaining graham crackers and sprinkle them over the top, or decorate with a few fresh mango slices.
  8. Chill to Set Cover the dish tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 6 hours, or ideally overnight, until the graham crackers soften and the cake is fully set. Alternatively, freeze for 2-3 hours for a firmer, ice-cream-like texture.
