Donuts

Overview

Homemade Yeast Donuts are light, airy, and fluffy, requiring a basic enriched dough that must be proofed (allowed to rise) twice. Once fried, they are often coated in granulated sugar, cinnamon sugar, or a simple glaze. Achieving the perfect texture depends on the frying temperature and the proper handling of the dough.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • All-Purpose Flour (3 cups, plus extra)
  • Active Dry Yeast (2 1/4 tsp)
  • Warm Milk (1 cup)
  • Sugar (1/4 cup)
  • Eggs (2 large, room temperature)
  • Butter (1/4 cup, melted and cooled)
  • Salt (1 tsp)
  • Vegetable Oil or Canola Oil (for deep frying)
  • Granulated Sugar or Powdered Sugar (for coating)

Steps:

  1. Activate the Yeast In a large bowl or stand mixer, combine the warm milk and 1 teaspoon of the sugar. Sprinkle the yeast over the top and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  2. Mix the Dough Add the remaining sugar, eggs, melted butter, and salt to the yeast mixture. Gradually mix in the flour until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Knead and First Proof Kneane the dough for about 5-8 minutes until it is smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, turn to coat, cover with plastic wrap, and let rise in a warm place for 1 to 1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.
  4. Shape the Donuts Gently punch down the dough. Turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and roll it out to 1/2-inch thickness. Use a donut cutter (or two different sized circular cutters) to cut out the donut rings and donut holes.
  5. Second Proof Carefully transfer the cut donuts to a parchment-lined baking sheet. Cover lightly and let them rise for a second time for 30-45 minutes, or until visibly puffy (this step is essential for airy texture).
  6. Heat the Oil In a deep pot or Dutch oven, heat the oil to **350°F (175°C)**. Use a thermometer to maintain the temperature.
  7. Fry the Donuts Working in batches, carefully drop 2-3 donuts into the hot oil. Fry for 1-2 minutes per side until golden brown. Adjust the heat as necessary to keep the oil temperature steady.
  8. Drain and Finish Remove the donuts with a slotted spoon and place them on a wire rack lined with paper towels to drain excess oil. While still warm, toss them immediately in granulated sugar or coat them in your desired glaze. Serve fresh.
