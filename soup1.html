Crab & Corn soup

Overview

Crab and Corn Soup is a quick and satisfying Chinese-inspired soup known for its smooth texture and sweet, savory flavor profile. It's typically thickened using cornstarch, giving it a velvety body, and finished by swirling in beaten egg, creating beautiful "egg ribbons" throughout the broth.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • Chicken Broth (6 cups)
  • Canned Cream-Style Corn (1 can)
  • Crab Meat (200g, flaked)
  • Cornstarch (3 tbsp)
  • Water (3 tbsp, for slurry)
  • Eggs (2 large, beaten)
  • Ginger (1 tsp, minced)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • White Pepper (1/2 tsp)
  • Sesame Oil (1 tsp)
  • Green Onions/Scallions (2 stalks)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Broth In a large pot, bring the chicken broth to a boil over medium-high heat. Add the canned cream-style corn and the grated ginger. Reduce the heat and simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Season the Soup Stir in the flaked crab meat. Season the soup with salt and white pepper to taste. If the crab meat is unsalted, you may need more salt.
  3. Thicken the Soup In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and 3 tablespoons of water to create a smooth slurry. While the soup is simmering, slowly pour the cornstarch slurry into the pot in a steady stream, stirring continuously until the soup thickens to your desired consistency.
  4. Add Egg Ribbons Bring the soup back to a gentle simmer. Slowly drizzle the lightly beaten eggs into the soup in a thin, steady stream while stirring the soup rapidly in one direction. The egg should immediately cook into fine ribbons.
  5. Finish and Serve Remove the soup from the heat immediately. Stir in the sesame oil. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish generously with sliced green onions before serving hot.
