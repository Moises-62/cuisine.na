Mashed Potato

Overview

Mashed potatoes are a simple yet essential side dish made by boiling potatoes until tender, then mashing them while incorporating butter, milk, and seasonings. The best results come from using high-starch potatoes (like Russet or Idaho) and warming the dairy ingredients before mixing them in.

Preparation Details

Ingredients:

  • Potatoes (1 kg / 4 large, Russet or Yukon Gold)
  • Butter (1/2 cup / 1 stick, unsalted)
  • Milk or Heavy Cream (1/2 cup)
  • Salt (for boiling and seasoning)
  • Black Pepper (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Potatoes Peel the potatoes and cut them into roughly equal-sized chunks (about 1.5 to 2 inches). This ensures they cook evenly.
  2. Boil the Potatoes Place the potato chunks in a large pot and cover them with cold water. Add 1 tablespoon of salt to the water. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 15 to 20 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender.
  3. Drain Thoroughly Drain the potatoes completely in a colander. Return the drained potatoes to the empty pot and place the pot back on the stove over very low heat for 1 minute, shaking gently. This helps evaporate excess moisture, which is key to fluffy mash.
  4. Warm the Dairy While the potatoes drain, gently heat the milk (or cream) and butter in a small saucepan until the butter is melted and the milk is warm. Do not let it boil.
  5. Mash the Potatoes Using a potato masher, ricer, or food mill, mash the hot potatoes until they reach your desired consistency. Avoid over-mashing, as this can make the potatoes gummy.
  6. Combine Gently Gradually add the warm milk and butter mixture to the mashed potatoes while stirring gently with a spoon or spatula.
  7. Season Finish Season the mashed potatoes with salt and black pepper to taste. Mix gently one last time. Serve immediately while hot.
